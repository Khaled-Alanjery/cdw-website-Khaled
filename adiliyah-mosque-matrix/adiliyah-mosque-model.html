Jamiʿ al-ʿAdiliyya, Aleppo — Damage Assessment, CAD Geometry & Real Site Conditions

Rebuilt strictly from primary sources: the 2019 Rapid Damage Assessment (Museum for Islamic Art, Berlin / Syrian Heritage Archive Project), the site's own CAD survey (FLS1615), and live pulls from NOAA, NASA, and open elevation/astronomy APIs. No estimated or synthetic values are included — where a real value could not be sourced (air quality), the layer is omitted rather than approximated.

Documented Timeline, 1556–2019

Every point below is a dated, credited photograph or document cited in the Rapid Damage Assessment report, plus two construction dates corroborated by Wikipedia. Hover a point for its source.

Damage Zones as of 25.06.2019

Condition class (CC) and urgency class (UC) as assigned in the report, following BS EN 16096:2012. Urgency deadlines below are computed directly from the report's own stated response windows (UC3 = 3 months, UC2 = 1 year from the report date) — not predictions.

Real Temperature Record — NOAA Station SYE00147760 ("ALEPPO, SY")

Ground station 2.6km from the site. Every point is an actual daily reading (metric, GHCN daily-summaries). The station reported densely before the war and resumed sparsely afterward — nothing between is invented.

⚠ Station silent 2012-11-14 → 2019-11-20 (2,562 days). This gap covers the entire documented conflict and damage period for this building — the absence of ground weather data is itself part of the record.

Invisible Site Factors

Real, sourced measurements only. AQI was checked against every reachable air-quality API and no historical data exists for this location in this environment — that layer has been omitted rather than filled in.

CAD Cross-Validation — FLS1615 vs. Damage Report

Geometry measured directly from the DXF (units confirmed in-file via a scale bar reading "0 / 5 / 10 m"), checked against the report's own stated dimensions.
